Judicial Review for Canadian
Immigration Decisions

A Judicial Review is a legal process through which applicants can seek the court to review some of the decisions of the Canadian authorities on their immigration. When an application is refused or procedural concerns arise, Judicial Review Canada might offer a chance to evaluate the decision made was fair and made according to applicable laws and procedures.

Grounds for Judicial Review Canada Applications

Procedural Fairness Concerns

Identify situations where fairness, transparency, or proper procedures may not have been followed.

Errors in Decision Making

Review cases where there was factual errors, unreasonable conclusions or misapplication of regulations.

Immigration Application Refusals

Determine refusals of visas, permits, residency applications and other immigration matters.

Supporting Documentation

Present any relevant records, rejection letters, and documents to support the review request.

Legal Timelines Compliance

Make sure that the applications are filed on the time limits set by Canadian courts.

Valid Review Grounds

Prove recognized grounds for judicial review Canada through supporting facts and circumstances.
